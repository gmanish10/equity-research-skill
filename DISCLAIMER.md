## The framing

This skill defaults to an **aggressive-growth, higher-than-average risk tolerance** lens. This framing may not be appropriate for:
- Retirees or those approaching retirement
- Investors with short time horizons for the money at risk
- Anyone who cannot afford losses
- Those saving for imminent life goals (house down payment, tuition, emergencies)

If any of those describe you, do not use this skill's aggressive-growth outputs as a basis for investment decisions.

## Specific risk warnings

### Individual stocks
Individual stocks can lose 100% of their value. Concentrated positions can produce outsized losses. Past performance does not predict future returns.

### Options
Options can expire worthless. Long options have defined risk; short options can have unlimited risk. Options are leverage and amplify both gains and losses.

### Leveraged ETFs
Leveraged ETFs (2×, 3×) reset daily. Over longer holding periods, they can lose value even when the underlying index is flat or up due to volatility decay. They are designed for tactical, short-term use. Examples: TQQQ, SOXL, FAS, SPXU, SQQQ.

### Small-cap and thematic names
Smaller, less liquid, or narrative-driven names can move violently in both directions. Size accordingly.

### Concentrated portfolios
A portfolio with few positions or heavy sector concentration carries more specific risk than a diversified portfolio. The aggressive-growth framing explicitly trades diversification for conviction. This is a deliberate choice with real consequences.

## Data limitations

- Yahoo Finance data is delayed approximately 15 minutes
- Historical data may have inconsistencies or gaps
- Analyst estimates change frequently and may be stale
- Social sentiment indicators are lagging and can be manipulated
- SEC filings are primary sources but not always up to date

## Accuracy limitations

This skill uses Claude, a large language model, to synthesize data. While it is instructed to quantify everything and show its work, LLM outputs can:
- Contain factual errors
- Misinterpret data
- Generate plausible-sounding but incorrect numbers
- Miss relevant context

Always verify critical numbers and facts from primary sources (company filings, data providers, qualified professionals) before making investment decisions.
